RISWIS Phase 4B — Fatigue Domain Controlled Sensitivity Test

Purpose:
Evaluate tier-weighted ranking behavior under near-semantic collisions using a controlled fatigue-related T1/T2/T3 document trio within the full active corpus.

Controlled Documents:
doc_110 = T1 (formal fatigue explanation)
doc_111 = T2 (neutral fatigue summary)
doc_112 = T3 (casual fatigue explanation)

Active Corpus Context:
Full manifest active during testing (24 documents total). Older T1/T2/T3 documents remained eligible for retrieval.

--------------------------------------------------
Baseline Runs (T1 = 1.5, T2 = 1.0, T3 = 0.7)
--------------------------------------------------

Query: always tired but sleeping
Raw semantic winner: doc_111 (T2, sim=0.716)
Weighted winner: doc_110 (T1, weighted=0.928)
Classification: borderline override / sensitivity

Query: tired all the time need sleep or water
Raw semantic winner: doc_112 (T3, sim=0.871)
Weighted winner: doc_110 (T1, weighted=1.003)
Classification: questionable override / strong sensitivity

Query: body tired all day
Raw semantic winner: doc_111 (T2, sim=0.690)
Weighted winner: doc_110 (T1, weighted=0.878)
Classification: borderline override / sensitivity

Observed secondary behavior:
Older T1 documents (doc_001, doc_005, doc_107) rose in weighted rank despite lower semantic similarity, indicating broader multiplier influence across corpus.

--------------------------------------------------
Sensitivity Runs (T1 = 1.3, T2 = 1.0, T3 = 0.7)
--------------------------------------------------

Query: always tired but sleeping
Raw semantic winner: doc_111 (T2, sim=0.716)
Weighted winner: doc_110 (T1, weighted=0.804)

Query: tired all the time need sleep or water
Raw semantic winner: doc_112 (T3, sim=0.871)
Weighted winner: doc_110 (T1, weighted=0.869)

Query: body tired all day
Raw semantic winner: doc_111 (T2, sim=0.690)
Weighted winner: doc_110 (T1, weighted=0.761)

Sensitivity interpretation:
Reducing T1 from 1.5 to 1.3 softened weighted margins but did not change final rank outcomes.

--------------------------------------------------
Phase 4B Interpretation
--------------------------------------------------

Phase 4B confirms that RISWIS policy weighting remains dominant under near-semantic collisions.

The T1 document consistently moved from raw_rank=3 to weighted_rank=1 across all fatigue-domain queries.

This indicates that:
- trust weighting remains stable under paraphrase variation
- semantic winners can still be overtaken by policy
- multiplier influence extends beyond the controlled trio into full corpus retrieval

Conclusion:
Phase 4B produced a stable sensitivity signal and is complete for fatigue-domain evaluation.
