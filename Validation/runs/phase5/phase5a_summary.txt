RISWIS Phase 5A Summary
Cluster Competition Validation

Objective:
Phase 5A tested whether RISWIS governance remains interpretable when multiple semantically similar documents compete across trust tiers.

Design:
A clustered fatigue corpus was introduced using controlled trust tiers.

Batch 1:
Initial cluster introduced with:
- T1 = 2 documents
- T2 = 1 document
- T3 = 2 documents

Batch 2:
Counter-cluster introduced with stronger lexical similarity in lower-tier phrasing:
- T1 = 2 additional documents
- T2 = 1 additional document
- T3 = 2 additional documents

Queries used across runs:
- persistent fatigue evaluation
- prolonged exhaustion
- tired for long periods
- poor sleep fatigue

Observed Result:
RISWIS governance remained fully visible under clustered semantic competition.

Across repeated runs:
- raw semantic winners remained observable
- weighted rank changes remained deterministic
- delta movement remained explainable

Primary finding:
Repeated T1 presence produced measurable saturation of weighted top ranks.

Batch 1 finding:
A semantically strongest T2 candidate fell after weighting when multiple T1 documents competed closely.

Batch 2 finding:
Even after adding stronger query-close lower-tier phrasing, repeated T1 documents continued to dominate weighted top ranks.

Meaning:
Phase 5A established that RISWIS behavior under cluster pressure remains interpretable, but trust clusters can collectively suppress lower-tier candidates even when semantic similarity remains strong.

Phase significance:
This extends RISWIS beyond isolated override behavior into clustered retrieval pressure analysis.
