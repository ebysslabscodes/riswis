RISWIS Phase 5B Summary
Query Pressure Validation

Phase 5B tested whether RISWIS ranking behavior remains interpretable when query wording changes across an unchanged clustered corpus.

Unlike Phase 5A, no new documents were added and no manifest changes were made. The purpose was to isolate query variation as the only changing variable.

Six fatigue-related queries were run across formal, casual, symptom-based, and threshold-oriented phrasing.

What was observed:

1. Final weighted rankings consistently ended with T1 documents occupying the top positions.

2. Raw semantic rankings varied significantly depending on wording.

3. Formal medical wording produced relatively stable raw ordering, with T1 documents already near the top before weighting.

4. Casual or emotionally broad wording produced much larger raw rank dispersion, with some T1 documents beginning as low as raw ranks 12 through 14 before recovering through weighting.

5. One query ("feeling drained every day") produced the first clear raw T2 winner:
   doc_111 ranked first in raw similarity but fell to weighted rank five after T1 weighting was applied.

Why this matters:

Phase 5B shows that RISWIS does not simply freeze one answer.

The semantic layer still responds to wording differences.

What remains visible is how governance changes final order.

This means raw semantic preference and final weighted preference can diverge while remaining fully inspectable.

Real-world interpretation:

A person asking:
"persistent fatigue evaluation"

and another asking:
"why am I tired all the time"

may trigger different raw semantic matches.

RISWIS shows exactly how trusted sources recover or remain stable under those wording changes rather than hiding rank corrections.

Important technical implication:

At current multipliers:

T1 = 1.5
T2 = 1.0
T3 = 0.7

T1 weighting is strong enough that moderate semantic gaps usually do not survive final ranking.

This does not mean lower-tier documents never lead raw ranking.

It means current governance strength remains dominant when semantic distances are not extreme.

Multiplier sensitivity observation:

Phase 5B results suggest that lower T1 values (for example 1.2) would likely alter some final outcomes.

This was not run experimentally in Phase 5B, but one observed case showed a T2 raw winner close enough that reduced T1 weighting would likely change final rank order.

Conclusion:

Phase 5B confirms that RISWIS governance remains visible under query variation.

The semantic layer changes.
The governance layer remains inspectable.
Final rank movement stays auditable.
